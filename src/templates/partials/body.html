Something something new industrial revolution. Coal is out, tech is in, it’s whole thing. Do you not watch the news? Which is why cities are willing to throw massive wads of cash in order to make their metropolis the next big American city.

They do this with maga-deals, no wait megadeals. Although the truth is the make as much sense. While there are benefits in attracting new businesses with these deals to your city, the numbers rarely add up. You see buddy, cities are handing out tax breaks like they’re free AOL CDs.

The biggest of these tech based megadeals was gifted to Foxconn by taxpaying Wisconsians. They dropped a smooth $4bn on bringing Foxconn to Mount Pleasant. Foxconn? More like Foxcon. Anyway, have a stab at this little question, will ya?

Mona's note that should be worked in somewhere: Just 12% of engineering jobs are held by women in the US. That number rises to 27% for computer and mathematical jobs but still, if you know someone working in tech, chances are, that's a man.
