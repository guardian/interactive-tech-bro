America’s cities all have the same dream these days: to be the next Silicon Valley. And to court Big Tech they have taken to showering some of the world’s most profitable companies with taxpayers’ cash.

On the surface the rationale seems obvious. Manufacturing is so 20th century – ship in the tech bros* and soon your rusty old mills will be designer lofts and dying high street will be throbbing with millennials, craft beer bars and cafes selling chai lattes and gluten-free flatbreads.

But while the mayor may get a photo op with Tim Cook or Elon Musk, do the numbers really add up for taxpayers? The record so far says no. And yet the handouts just seem to get bigger.

According to an analysis by Good Jobs First, the biggest of these tech-based megadeals so far was gifted to Foxconn, the Taiwanese maker of Apple’s iPhone, by taxpaying Wisconsinites. That deal could end up costing over $4bn.

What's all this Amazon business about??

Meanwhile, some of the US’s biggest cities are fighting hard to woo Amazon, which is looking for a home for its second headquarters. The cities that made it through to the second round are generally keeping quiet about how much taxpayers cash is on the table. But one contender, Newark, New Jersey, a city with an appalling crime rate and serious issues with its schools and transportation system, has reportedly offered $7bn. Looks like the most likely winner from the battle for HQ2 will be Amazon.
